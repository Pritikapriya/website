The Taste of Bihar

1. Thekua: Thekua is a popular Bihari sweet snack. It is a deep-fried cookie-like sweet made from wheat flour, jaggery (or sugar), and ghee. The dough is shaped into intricate designs before frying, giving it a unique texture and taste.

2. Khaja: Khaja is a traditional Bihari sweet that is prepared by deep frying thin layers of wheat flour dough. These layers are then coated with sugar syrup, making it crispy and sweet.

3. Pua: Pua is a dessert similar to pancakes, made from wheat flour, milk, sugar, and ripe bananas. The batter is fried in ghee until golden brown, resulting in a sweet and flavorful pancake-like treat.

4. Tilkut: Tilkut is a traditional sweet made from sesame seeds, jaggery, and ghee. The mixture is set into molds and allowed to cool, forming solid blocks. It's often prepared during Makar Sankranti, a harvest festival celebrated in Bihar.

5. Pittha: Pittha is a traditional Bihari dumpling made from rice flour or wheat flour. It can be sweet or savory and is stuffed with fillings like jaggery, coconut, sesame seeds, or lentils. Pittha is steamed or boiled and served with ghee or chutney.
